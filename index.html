Here's a time line of Aristides de Sousa Mendes' life:

  • 1885: born in Cabanas de Viriato, in Carregal do Sal, in the district of Viseu, Centro Region of Portugal, on July 19;
  • 1908: Sousa Mendes obtains a law degree from the University of Coimbra;
  • 1908: Begins his consular career. Initially posted in Zanzibar, Brazil, Spain, the United States, and Belgium;
  • 1938: Is assigned to the post of Consul-General of Bordeaux, France, with jurisdiction over the whole of the southwest of France;
  • 1939: Portuguese government sent Circular 14 to all Portuguese consuls, according to which issuing visas to Jews required special permission;
  • 1939: In violation to this order, Sousa Mendes issues visas without demanding prior permission, for which he is reprimanded;
  • 1940: Sousa Mendes continues to disobey this order, issuing approximately 30,000 visas and passports, of which around 10,000 were for Jews;
  • 1940: On June 24, the Portuguese government recalled Sousa Mendes to Portugal, which does not prevent him from issuing visas on his way;
  • 1940: Sousa Mendes is severly reprimanded for issuing visas, suspeding him for a year and blacklisting him and his family;
  • 1954: Sousa Mendes dies in poverty, still in disgrace with the government;
  • 1966: Sousa Mendes, in 1966, was among the earliest to be named Righteous Among the Nations by the Israeli Holocaust authority Yad Vashem;
  • 1987: Portugal awards him posthumously the Order of Liberty medal, rehabilitating him.
